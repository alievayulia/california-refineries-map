This map shows “pollution burden” scores calculated for selected Los Angeles-area census tracts by the California Environmental Protection Agency. Pollution burden reflects an average of exposures and environmental effects. Darker-colored areas, such as Wilmington, have higher scores, meaning they are more polluted and have higher prevalence of diseases such as asthma. Lighter-colored areas — such as more affluent Rancho Palos Verdes, about five miles away — are cleaner and healthier. Tap on an area of the map to see data for individual tracts.
